Marie curie

The only person to win a Nobel Prize in two scientific fields.

Here's a time line of The physicist and chemist:

  • 1867 Born in Warsaw, Poland.
  • 1891 Moved to Paris to study at the Sorbonne (University of Paris).
  • 1894 Married Pierre Curie, a French physicist.
  • 1896 Received her degree in physics and began researching uranium radiation, coining the term “radioactivity”.
  • 1898 Discovered polonium and radium alongside Pierre Curie.
  • 1903 Awarded The Nobel Prize in Physics for their work on radioactivity.
  • 1906 Pierre Curie tragically died in an accident.
  • 1908 Promoted to Professor at the Sorbonne.
  • 1911 Received her second Nobel Prize, this time in Chemistry, for radium and polonium discoveries.
  • 1914-1918 Developed mobile radiography units during World War I.
  • 1918-1934 Became director of the scientific department of the Radium Institute. Continued to research the chemistry of radioactive materials and their medical applications. Lectured internationally. Established scholarships for scientists.
  • 1934 Passed away from aplastic anemia due to radiation exposure.
  • 1995 Interred in the Panthéon in Paris, the first woman honored there for her own merits.
